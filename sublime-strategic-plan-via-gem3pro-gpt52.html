1. What Sublime Laser Is

Business model & current positioning

Core Offering

Contract laser micromachining of medical-device tubular components (nitinol, stainless steel, precious metals; also ceramic/glass) with an emphasis on tight tolerances and speed.

Femtosecond Laser

Material vaporization; minimal HAZ

  • OD: 0.008" – 0.313"
  • Wall: 0.0005" – 0.013"
  • Length: up to 120"
  • Kerf: 12–30 µm
  • Feature size: ~12 µm

Fiber Laser

Melting; faster throughput, larger envelopes

  • OD: 0.008" – 0.500"
  • Wall: 0.003" – 0.040"
  • Length: up to 120"
  • Kerf: 20–40 µm
  • Feature size: ~20 µm

Value Proposition

"Parts in days, not weeks or months"

  • Convenience + fastest possible turnaround.
  • Process capability in med devices.
  • Credibility: ISO 13485 certified (medical QMS).

Revenue Streams

  1. Prototype and R&D build orders (high margin, variable).
  2. Pilot and limited production (repeat orders).
  3. On-site "laser + engineer" rental (premium).
  4. Design/DFM services (sticky early engagement).
  5. Validated processes for specific indications.

2. Market Analysis

Primary Customers

  • Medical device companies developing neurovascular, cardiovascular, peripheral vascular devices.
  • R&D teams needing fast design iterations.
  • Supply chain teams needing qualified suppliers for pilot/production.

Macro Drivers

  • Continued growth in minimally invasive therapies (stents, thrombectomy).
  • More complex lattice/mesh geometries pushing demand toward ultrafast lasers.
  • OEM pressure to shorten development cycles.

Key Market Tension

Customers want "prototype speed" and "production discipline" simultaneously. Winning suppliers productize speed (standard quoting, design rules, capacity buffers) without sacrificing validation and documentation.

3. Competitor Landscape

A) Large Outsourced Manufacturers

Strengths: Scale, broad portfolio (laser + EDM + grind), global footprint.

Weaknesses: Slow quoting, high overhead, less flexible.

Sublime Strategy: Be faster and more specialized.

B) Specialized Job Shops

Strengths: Similar equipment, speed, expertise.

Weaknesses: Undifferentiated, capacity constraints.

Sublime Strategy: Validate processes & systematize speed.

C) In-house OEM Teams

Strengths: IP control, immediate iteration.

Weaknesses: Capital cost, staffing, limited surge capacity.

Sublime Strategy: "Rent a laser" program.

D) Adjacent Substitutes

Photochemical etching, Micro-EDM, Additive manufacturing.

Sublime Strategy: Focus on validated femto processes and traceability.

4. Strategic Diagnosis (SWOT)

Strengths

  • ISO 13485 Certification.
  • Positioning: Speed + Microns + Minimal HAZ.
  • Both Femto and Fiber offerings.
  • "Rent a laser" differentiator.

Weaknesses

  • Narrow public footprint of case studies.
  • Reliance on speed can be commoditized.
  • Scaling risk (capacity constraints vs lead-time promise).

Opportunities

  • Productize R&D-to-pilot handoff.
  • Become default supplier for component archetypes.
  • Digital quoting + DFM rules engine.
  • Expand into process validation services.

Threats

  • Large CMs adding ultrafast capacity.
  • Price pressure in commoditized patterns.
  • OEMs bringing femto capability in-house.

5. Strategic Pillars

North Star (3-Year): Become a go-to rapid-development and pilot-production partner known for fast iteration, validated repeatable processes, and data-rich documentation.

Pillar 1: "Speed System"

Goal: Quote-to-part in days, reliably.

  • Standardized intake & DFM rules.
  • Capacity buffering for rush R&D.
  • Fast metrology loops.

Pillar 2: Process Lock Packages

Goal: Convert prototypes to repeat business.

  • Prototype Pack: Basic notes + dim report.
  • Pilot Pack: Critical parameters, lot traceability.
  • Production Pack: Cp/Cpk, control plan.

Pillar 3: Adjacencies

Goal: Add value without "boiling the ocean".

  • Vetted partner network for electropolishing/passivation.
  • Component-ready deliveries.

Pillar 4: Engineer + Laser

Goal: Premium subscription service.

  • Co-location with Sublime tech.
  • Structured DOE blocks.
  • Optimized pattern parameters output.

6. Year-by-Year Plan

Year 1

Foundation + Commercial Clarity

Prove reliability and capture more R&D.

  • Segment Offering: Rapid Prototype vs. Pilot Builds vs. Dev Sprint.
  • Laser Tube Design Guide: Publish guidance for femto vs fiber.
  • Customer Portal: Lightweight RFQ and order tracking.
  • Standard Metrology: Define standard vs validation-ready reports.

KPIs: RFQ-to-quote time, On-time delivery, Repeat order rate.

Year 2

Scale + Differentiation

Own the R&D-to-pilot bridge.

  • Process Lock Launch: Sell Pilot/Production packs.
  • Specialization: Dominate neurovascular thin-wall nitinol patterns.
  • Partnered Finishing: Single PO for laser + finishing.
  • Content: Publish anonymized case studies.

KPIs: Revenue mix (pilot vs proto), Gross margin, Preferred supplier wins.

Year 3

Innovative Expansion

New business lines that leverage your core.

  • Innovation A: Instant DFM Rules Engine.
  • Innovation B: Micro-tube "platform processes" with guaranteed windows.
  • Innovation C: Regulatory-ready manufacturing documentation product.
  • Innovation D: Adjacent verticals (Aerospace/Fluidics) via separate lanes.
