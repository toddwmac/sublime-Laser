Market Drivers & Competitive Landscape

The "Micro" Revolution in Surgery

The medical device market is aggressively shifting toward minimally invasive procedures. This trend necessitates smaller, more durable, and geometrically complex components—specifically guidewires, stents, and catheter shafts.


  • Nitinol Dominance: Increasing reliance on shape-memory alloys (Nitinol) which are notoriously difficult to machine using traditional EDM or CNC methods.
  • Complexity Demands: Surgeons demand features invisible to the naked eye (micro-holes for drug delivery, cutting patterns for flexibility).
  • R&D Velocity: Competitor devices launch every quarter; manufacturers need rapid iteration capabilities.
