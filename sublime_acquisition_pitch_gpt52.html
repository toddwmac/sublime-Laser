Buyer problem

Vascular device innovation is constrained by prototype lead-times

In neurovascular/cardiovascular/peripheral vascular devices, tubular components often require micron-scale features, tight tolerances, and careful thermal control. When suppliers quote long lead-times, R&D schedules slip and NPI gates slow down. Sublime directly targets this pain with a “days, not weeks or months” value proposition.

Sublime solution

Rapid micromachining + on-demand iteration

Sublime offers (1) femtosecond laser machining (vaporization, “almost no HAZ”) for quality-critical microfeatures, (2) fiber laser machining (melting) for fast, accurate production, and (3) “Rent a Laser” / “Laser and Engineer For a Day” so teams can iterate design parameters on-site and compress the design loop.

Where the market is going

Trends that strengthen Sublime’s positioning

  • Acceleration of NPI cycles: OEMs want shorter iteration loops; Sublime’s “days not weeks/months” is aligned to this.
  • Miniaturization: demand for smaller tubular features increases value of 12–20 micron feature capability.
  • Material complexity: nitinol adoption in vascular devices increases need for specialized processing and fatigue-aware cutting.
  • Supplier consolidation + audit pressure: pushes work toward ISO-aligned vendors; Sublime highlights ISO 13485 presence on-site navigation and footer.

Non-financial diligence checklist

Technical & operational items

  • Capacity & redundancy: number of femtosecond and fiber systems; maintenance logs; uptime; spare parts.
  • Metrology: how microfeatures (12–20 microns) are verified; gage R&R; calibration program.
  • Process windows: for nitinol and SS; how HAZ/recast is controlled; cleaning/handling for long tubes (up to 120").
  • Documentation: SOPs, training, job travelers, revision control—especially for regulated customers.
Commercial diligence checklist

Customer stickiness & growth

  • Prototype → production conversion: evidence that R&D projects convert into repeat manufacturing orders.
  • “Rent a laser” economics: utilization, pricing model, and how often it leads to follow-on manufacturing work.
  • Lead time KPI: validate the “days” promise across sample jobs; on-time delivery performance.
  • Target segments: quantify exposure to neuro/cardiovascular/peripheral vascular programs.
