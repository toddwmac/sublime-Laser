Mission Statement

To be the premier rapid-response partner for precision laser-machined medical components, leveraging femtosecond and fiber laser technology to accelerate device innovation from concept to production — while expanding into adjacent markets and breakthrough manufacturing technologies.

Current State

Where We Are Today

Sublime Laser is a small, agile, ISO 13485-certified contract manufacturer specializing in femtosecond and fiber laser machining of tubular medical components (stents, flow diverters, aspiration catheters). The team of 4 serves R&D teams at neurovascular, cardiovascular, and peripheral vascular device companies, differentiating on speed (days vs. weeks) and a unique "Rent-a-Laser" offering.

3-Year Vision

Where We're Going

By 2027, Sublime Laser will be a 25+ person operation generating $8M+ revenue with three distinct business lines: (1) Core Contract Manufacturing, (2) Advanced Development Services with digital twin integration, and (3) a breakthrough Laser-as-a-Service (LaaS) platform. The company will serve medical, aerospace, and semiconductor markets from an expanded 12,000 sq ft facility.

Speed

Days, Not Months

While incumbent competitors quote 6–12 week lead times, Sublime delivers prototype components in days. For R&D teams on tight timelines, this is the #1 purchase driver. Each week saved in prototyping can be worth $50K–$200K in program acceleration.

Precision

Femtosecond-Class Quality

Near-zero heat affected zone means no recast layer, no microcracking, and preserved material properties — critical for Nitinol shape memory alloys. 12μm feature resolution is best-in-class for contract manufacturers.

Flexibility

Rent-a-Laser Model

A truly differentiated service: customers can rent laser time plus an expert technician for on-site iteration. This collapses weeks of design-build-test cycles into a single day, enabling real-time design optimization.

Expertise

Design Partnership

Beyond cutting, the team offers DFM (Design for Manufacturability) consultation, helping R&D engineers optimize stent patterns, strut widths, and connector geometries for both performance and manufacturability.

Innovation Frontier

Breakthrough initiatives that stretch Sublime Laser into transformative new territory.

Year 2–3

🤖 AI-Powered Autonomous Cutting

Machine learning models trained on thousands of cutting jobs to autonomously optimize laser parameters (power, speed, frequency, gas flow) for any new geometry/material combination. Reduces setup time from hours to minutes and enables "lights-out" 24/7 production.

Year 2–3

🌐 Digital Twin Process Simulation

Physics-based simulation engine that predicts cut quality, recoil pressure, heat distribution, and potential defects before a single pulse is fired. Customers can virtually test designs and receive manufacturability scores, dramatically reducing physical iteration cycles.

Year 3

☁️ Laser-as-a-Service (LaaS) Platform

A cloud-based platform where customers upload CAD files, configure material/tolerance specs, and receive instant pricing. AI performs automated DFM analysis. Orders are routed to available laser systems for optimal scheduling. Parts ship within 24–72 hours. Subscription tiers for volume customers.

Year 3

🧬 Bioresorbable Device Manufacturing

Develop femtosecond laser parameters for cutting bioresorbable polymers (PLLA, PCL, Mg alloys) used in next-generation scaffolds that dissolve in the body. This emerging $2B+ market requires cold ablation to avoid material degradation — a perfect fit for femtosecond technology.

Year 2–3

⚡ Hybrid Additive-Subtractive Manufacturing

Combine femtosecond laser cutting with selective laser sintering (SLS) or directed energy deposition (DED) to create components impossible to make with either process alone. Example: 3D-printed Nitinol stent body with femtosecond-cut micro-features for drug elution pores.

Year 3+

🔬 Nano-Patterning for Biosensors

Leverage femtosecond laser surface nano-structuring to create biosensor-embedded medical devices. Surface patterns at sub-micron scale can enable drug-release monitoring, blood flow sensing, or tissue integration markers — opening a new category of "smart" implantable devices.

Risk Assessment & Mitigation

Key risks to plan execution and strategies to mitigate them.

HIGH

Key Person Dependency

Risk: CEO/Founder Todd Turnlund holds critical customer relationships and technical knowledge. Loss would severely impact operations.
Mitigation: Document all processes. Cross-train team members. Hire senior engineer capable of assuming technical leadership. Establish advisory board.

HIGH

Customer Concentration

Risk: With a small customer base, loss of a top customer could significantly impact revenue.
Mitigation: Aggressive customer acquisition (target 40+ by Y1 end). Diversify into non-medical markets. Build long-term supply agreements.

MEDIUM

Talent Acquisition

Risk: Difficulty hiring skilled laser technicians and engineers in competitive Utah market.
Mitigation: Partner with local universities (U of U, BYU). Offer competitive comp + equity. Develop internal training program. Consider remote engineering roles.

MEDIUM

Technology Obsolescence

Risk: Rapid advancement in laser technology could make current equipment less competitive.
Mitigation: Maintain relationships with laser OEMs. Budget for continuous technology refresh. Monitor competing technologies (waterjet, EDM, additive).

MEDIUM

Scaling Too Fast

Risk: Rapid growth could strain quality systems, leading to customer issues and regulatory non-compliance.
Mitigation: Quality-first culture. Hire quality team ahead of growth. Gate new customer onboarding to capacity. Regular management review of quality metrics.

LOW

Market Downturn

Risk: Economic recession reduces medical device R&D spending.
Mitigation: Medical devices are relatively recession-resistant. Market diversification (aerospace, semi) provides counter-cyclical balance. Maintain lean operations.

Strategic Priority Framework

🎯 Priority 1: Speed as a Brand

Speed is Sublime's moat. Every decision should be evaluated through the lens of "does this maintain or enhance our speed advantage?" Invest in automation, parallel processing, and streamlined workflows to keep turnaround under 5 business days even as volume scales.

🎯 Priority 2: Customer Intimacy

As the team grows, maintain the direct-access, partnership-oriented customer experience. Every customer should feel like they have a dedicated engineering partner, not just a vendor. Assign named engineers to key accounts.

🎯 Priority 3: Technology Leadership

Continue to invest in the most advanced laser technology available. Be the first contract manufacturer to offer each new capability (attosecond, hybrid additive, nano-patterning). Publish research to build thought leadership.

🎯 Priority 4: Platform Thinking

Evolve from a job shop to a platform business. The LaaS model, digital twin, and AI DFM tools create scalable, recurring revenue streams that are not linearly tied to headcount or machine hours.
