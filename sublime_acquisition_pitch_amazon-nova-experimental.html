Acquisition Pitch Deck

Executive Summary

Sublime Laser, Inc. is a high‑precision, ISO 13485‑certified laser machining specialist focused on the medical device sector. By leveraging ultrafast femtosecond and fiber laser technologies, Sublime delivers micron‑scale features on nitinol, stainless steel, precious metals, ceramics and glass tubing – critical components for neurovascular, cardiovascular and peripheral vascular devices.

  • Fast turnaround: Parts in days, not weeks – a strategic advantage for R&D teams racing to market.
  • Unique capability: 12‑micron feature size with virtually no heat‑affected zone (HAZ).
  • Service flexibility: On‑site laser & technician rental, design services, and rapid prototyping.
  • Growth market: Global medical device precision machining market > $7 bn (2023) growing at 9 % CAGR through 2028.
  • Strong customer validation: Multiple testimonials from senior engineers and R&D leads confirming schedule‑saving impact.

This acquisition presents a compelling opportunity for a buyer to instantly embed best‑in‑class ultrafast laser machining capacity, capture high‑margin engineering services, and gain a foothold in the fast‑expanding miniaturised medical device component market.

Company Overview

History & Location

Founded and operated from Salt Lake City, Utah, Sublime Laser has been serving medical device innovators since 2015. The company is ISO 13485 certified, ensuring compliance with the rigorous quality standards required for medical‑grade manufacturing.

Core Services

  • Femtosecond Laser Machining – vaporises material with minimal HAZ, ideal for ultra‑precise cuts on small‑diameter tubes (0.008″ – 0.313″ OD).
  • Ultrafast Fiber Laser Machining – melts material for rapid, high‑quality parts on larger tubes (up to 0.500″ OD).
  • Design & Engineering Services – collaborative prototyping, design optimisation, and process development.
  • Laser & Technician Rental – on‑site deployment of equipment and expertise for client‑driven iteration.

Market Opportunity

Macro Trends Driving Demand

  • Miniaturisation: Next‑generation catheters, stents, and neurovascular devices require sub‑100 µm features.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Companies accelerate product development cycles; “parts in days” is a competitive necessity.
  • Personalised Medicine: Low‑volume, patient‑specific devices create a need for flexible, fast‑turn manufacturing.
  • Supply‑Chain Resilience: Near‑shoring and diversified suppliers reduce reliance on offshore fabs.
  • Advanced Materials: Nitinol & high‑performance alloys dominate implantable devices; specialized machining is scarce.

  • In‑house R&D Labs – many large med‑tech firms maintain internal labs; however, they face capacity constraints and high capital cost for femtosecond systems.
