Sublime Laser, Inc.

3-Year Strategic Business Plan (2026-2029)

Executive Summary

Sublime Laser holds a unique position in precision medical device manufacturing with proprietary femtosecond laser technology that delivers superior precision and speed. This plan charts a course to transform from a specialized contract manufacturer into a diversified laser technology company with expanded market reach, product lines, and innovative service offerings.

Vision: Become the preferred precision laser manufacturing partner for medical devices globally, while expanding into adjacent high-margin markets.

Section 1: Current State Analysis

Business Overview

  • Core Competency: Femtosecond and fiber laser machining of tubular medical components
  • Primary Markets: Neurovascular, cardiovascular, and peripheral vascular device manufacturers
  • Key Differentiators:
    • Ultra-fast turnaround (days vs. weeks/months)
    • Minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ) with femtosecond technology
    • ISO 13485 certified medical device manufacturing
    • Deep expertise in nitinol and stainless steel
  • Revenue Model: Contract manufacturing, laser rental, engineering services
  • Team Size: 4 core staff (founder, principal engineer, supervisor, technician)

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  1. Technical Excellence: Proprietary femtosecond laser capabilities (12-micron features)
  2. Agility: Small team enables rapid prototyping and customization
  3. Medical Device Expertise: ISO certification and deep domain knowledge
  4. Relationship-Based: Long-standing R&D partnerships
  5. Location Advantage: Salt Lake City medical device cluster

Weaknesses

  1. Limited Scale: 4-person team constrains revenue
  2. Single Market Focus: Dependence on vascular devices
  3. Service-Based Model: Labor-intensive, limited scalability
  4. Low Digital Presence: Minimal blog/outdated content
  5. No Products: Pure services with no proprietary products
  6. Capital Intensity: High equipment costs

Market Opportunities

  1. Orthopedic Devices & Implants
  2. Ophthalmology (Lens implants)
  3. Dental Implants (Customization)
  4. Non-Medical: Electronics, optics, aerospace
  5. Direct-to-Market Products (White-label)
  6. Licensing Model

Competitive Threats

  1. Larger Contract Manufacturers (Precision Castparts, Medtronic)
  2. Traditional Machining (CNC, EDM improvements)
  3. Emerging Laser Companies (Cheaper systems)
  4. Insourcing Pressure
  5. Consolidation in the market

Section 2: 3-Year Strategic Plan

Phase 1: Years 1-2 - Foundation & Market Expansion

GOAL 1.1: Scale Core Business Strategically

Objective: Increase revenue 150-200% while maintaining quality.

  • Hire 2-3 additional technicians.
  • Implement MES system to optimize scheduling.
  • Develop standardized workflows for high-volume components.
  • Establish Volume Pricing Tiers.
  • Target: $500K-$750K additional revenue by end of Year 2.

GOAL 1.2: Expand Market Verticals (Medical)

Objective: Reduce dependence on vascular devices; enter 2 new medical markets.

Orthopedic & Spine

Identify top 20 manufacturers. Develop case studies for porous scaffolds/spinal implants.

Target: 20% of revenue.

Ophthalmic Devices

Focus on IOL components. Highlight minimal HAZ advantage.

Target: 10% of revenue.

Dental Implants

Partner on precision-machined prosthetic components with major OEMs.

Target: Pilot projects with 2-3 OEMs.

GOAL 1.3: Digital Transformation & Market Presence

  • Rebrand Blog: Create high-value content (Case studies, Technical deep dives).
  • LinkedIn Strategy: CEO thought leadership, company page engagement.
  • Website Overhaul: Improve UX, add portfolio and testimonials.
  • Target: 3x increase in qualified inbound leads.

GOAL 1.4: Operational Excellence

  • Implement Quality Management System procedures.
  • Equipment Optimization for speed/precision balance.
  • Establish preferred vendor supply chain.
  • Target: Gross margin improvement from ~55% to 62-65%.

Phase 2: Years 2-3 - Innovation & Product Development

GOAL 2.1: Enter Non-Medical Precision Markets

  • Electronics & Semiconductors: Thermal management systems, micro-channel heat sinks.
  • Aerospace & Defense: Fuel system components, sensor housings. Work toward NADCAP.
  • R&D Equipment: Expand "Laser and Engineer for a Day" model.

GOAL 2.2: Develop Proprietary Product Line

  • Precision Component Library: 20-30 standardized components (stents, tubes).
  • Micro-Featured Component Kits: Ready-for-assembly kits.
  • Licensing & IP: Patent applications for proprietary applications.

GOAL 2.3: Expand Service Offerings

  • Advanced Design Services ($150-200/hr).
  • On-Site Manufacturing Partnerships (extended engineer on-site).
  • Training & Certification Programs.

GOAL 2.4: Strategic Partnerships

  • Distribution Partnerships (white-label).
  • OEM Partnerships (preferred supplier status).
  • Technology Partnerships (Coherent, Spectra-Physics).

GOAL 2.5: Selective Equipment & Capacity Expansion

  • Explore leasing models rather than purchasing.
  • Evaluate facility expansion within Salt Lake City cluster.
  • Invest in robotic material handling automation.

Phase 3: Years 3+ - Scaling & Optimization

Goal 3.1: Achieve $2.5M - $3.5M revenue and 45% EBITDA margin.

Section 3: Innovative Business Model Extensions

Pillar 1: Platform-Based Model ("LaserNet")

Concept: Cloud-Enabled Manufacturing Platform connecting device manufacturers with precision laser capabilities.

Features: Design Upload Portal, Virtual Prototyping, Real-Time Tracking, AI Quality Verification.

Target: $200K+ annual revenue by Year 3.

Pillar 2: Subscription Model ("PaaS")

Concept: "Precision-as-a-Service". Device manufacturers pay monthly for reserved slots and engineering access.

Model: $10K-25K monthly recurring revenue per customer.

Target: $600K-$1.8M annual recurring revenue.

Pillar 3: Equipment Rental ("LaserShare")

Concept: Spin-out sister company for regional equipment rental and support.

Target: Lease 6-10 units by Year 3 ($600K-$1.2M revenue).

Pillar 4: Materials Science Partnership

Concept: Partner with labs to develop next-gen alloys (Nitinol formulations) optimized for lasers.

Target: Royalty on alloy sales, premium pricing.

Pillar 5: Digital Twin & Predictive Mfg

Concept: AI-Powered Precision Optimization Engine. Camera-based inspection and parameter optimization.

Target: SaaS platform licensing ($150K-300K revenue).

Pillar 6: Accelerator ("LaserStart")

Concept: Incubation for early-stage medical device startups.

Offering: Subsidized manufacturing for equity stake (5-10%).

Target: Portfolio value $2M-5M by Year 3.
