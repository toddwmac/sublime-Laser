3-Year Strategic Business Plan

Sublime Laser, Inc. — Precision Medical Device Manufacturing

Sublime Laser holds a unique position in precision medical device manufacturing with proprietary femtosecond laser technology that delivers superior precision and speed. This plan charts a course to transform from a specialized contract manufacturer into a diversified laser technology company with expanded market reach, product lines, and innovative service offerings.

Vision: Become the preferred precision laser manufacturing partner for medical devices globally, while expanding into adjacent high-margin markets.
1

Current State Analysis

Business Overview

  • Core Competency: Femtosecond and fiber laser machining of tubular medical components
  • Primary Markets: Neurovascular, cardiovascular, and peripheral vascular device manufacturers
  • Key Differentiators:
    • Ultra-fast turnaround (days vs. weeks/months)
    • Minimal heat-affected zone (HAZ) with femtosecond technology
    • ISO 13485 certified medical device manufacturing
    • Deep expertise in nitinol and stainless steel
  • Revenue Model: Contract manufacturing, laser rental, engineering services
  • Team Size: 4 core staff (founder, principal engineer, supervisor, technician)

💪 Strengths

  1. Technical Excellence: Proprietary femtosecond laser capabilities for precision work (12-micron features)
  2. Agility: Small team enables rapid prototyping and customization
  3. Medical Device Expertise: ISO certification and deep domain knowledge
  4. Relationship-Based: Long-standing R&D partnerships with device manufacturers
  5. Location Advantage: Salt Lake City medical device cluster

⚡ Weaknesses

  1. Limited Scale: 4-person team constrains revenue and growth
  2. Single Market Focus: Heavy dependence on vascular device manufacturers
  3. Service-Based Model: Labor-intensive, limited scalability
  4. Low Digital Presence: Minimal blog content; outdated website content (blog posts generic)
  5. No Products: Pure services/contract manufacturing with no proprietary products
  6. Capital Intensity: High equipment costs limit rapid expansion

🌟 Market Opportunities

  1. Orthopedic Devices: Expanding use of precision-machined components
  2. Ophthalmology: Precision micro-machining for lens implants, catheters
  3. Dental Implants: Growing customization trend
  4. Non-Medical Markets: Electronics, optics, aerospace precision machining
  5. Geographic Expansion: Limited international presence
  6. Automation: Minimal automation in current workflows
  7. Direct-to-Market Products: White-label or branded component lines
  8. Licensing Model: License technology to larger contract manufacturers

🔴 Competitive Threats

  1. Larger Contract Manufacturers: Precision Castparts, Medtronic internal manufacturing
  2. Traditional Machining: CNC, EDM alternatives improving
  3. Emerging Laser Companies: New entrants with cheaper fiber laser systems
  4. Insourcing Pressure: Device makers developing internal capabilities
  5. Consolidation: Larger firms acquiring specialized capabilities

Innovative Business Model Extensions

1

Platform-Based Model

"LaserNet" — Cloud-Enabled Manufacturing Platform
▼

Concept: Build a B2B platform connecting device manufacturers with precision laser capabilities

Features

  • Design Upload Portal: Customers submit designs; instant quote generation using AI/ML
  • Virtual Prototyping: Simulate laser machining results before production
  • Real-Time Tracking: Live dashboard of production progress
  • Quality Verification: Automated inspection data integration (SEM imaging, dimensional verification)
  • Marketplace: Connect smaller contract manufacturers with outsourced precision work

Revenue Model

  • Platform subscription ($5K-10K/month per user)
  • Transaction fees (5-8% of component orders)
🎯 Target: $200K+ annual revenue by Year 3
💰 Investment: $150K-250K software development
2

Custom Component Subscription Model

"Precision-as-a-Service" (PaaS) Model
▼

Concept: Device manufacturers pay monthly for access to precision manufacturing capacity and pre-developed component library

Offering

  • Reserved production slots (e.g., "100 components/month")
  • Priority access to design engineering team
  • Tiered pricing based on volume and complexity
  • Flexible contracts (3-36 months)

Target Customers: Mid-size medical device companies lacking in-house precision capabilities

Revenue Model

  • Monthly recurring revenue: $10K-25K per customer
  • Target: 4-6 PaaS customers by Year 3
🎯 Target: $600K-$1.8M annual recurring revenue
💰 Investment: $50K (system setup, customer onboarding)

Critical Success Factors & Risks

Critical Success Factors

  1. Talent Acquisition & Retention
    Key challenge: Attracting experienced laser technicians in Salt Lake City
    Mitigation: Offer competitive salaries, equity opportunities, training programs
  2. Market Expansion Execution
    Key challenge: Entering new markets while maintaining quality and service
    Mitigation: Structured pilot approach, dedicated business development resources
  3. Product Development & Timing
    Key challenge: Developing products that gain market traction
    Mitigation: Validate concepts with customers early, iterate rapidly
  4. Capital Management
    Key challenge: Funding growth without overextending
    Mitigation: Prioritize recurring revenue models, consider strategic funding
  5. Operational Excellence
    Key challenge: Scaling quality and consistency as volume increases
    Mitigation: Invest in automation and systems early
